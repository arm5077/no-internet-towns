Some towns have nothing to worry about. In the 2013 American Community Survey, 14 percent of cities and towns showed Internet connectivity rates over 90 percent.

Unsurprisingly, these include some of the wealthiest ZIP codes in the U.S.

Towns with best Internet access

Others aren't so lucky. A slew of economically depressed cities posted rates hovering around 50-60 percent, even though multiple providers offer high-speed Internet service there.

Towns with worst Internet access

Whether or not a town sees widespread Internet usage is strongly correlated with the percentage of its residents living in poverty.

There's a lesser (but still significant) connection to how many residents have college degrees.

One factor that appears largely irrelevant? The median age in a community. Grandma and Grandpa are on the Internet too, after all.

Source: 2013 American Community Survey 1-year estimates
